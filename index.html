Setting the Context

LearnShift India is an education conclave that brings together a small intimate group of individuals who are ever willing to share ideas, build networks and work together to ensure lasting educational change. Since its conception in 2012, this conference has involved a diverse gathering of high-level leaders, thinkers and pioneers who seek to push beyond the current educational paradigm and harness new approaches that deliver better learning for all young people.

LearnShift India is organized by R.N. Podar School, Mumbai and co-curated by Simon Breakspear, a leading international thinker on education reform and Mrs. Avnita Bir, Director-Principal, R.N. Podar School.

What's in Store

From addressing big questions around Equity, Inclusion, Teacher Quality, and Technology in its first year, to showcasing case-studies of innovations that sought to create a Shift in Access, Pedagogy, and Educators in the second year, LSI moves on this year to bring insights from the classroom and blends it with ideas from around the world to challenge our current schooling system.

The big questions it seeks to address are around implementing and managing change as a leader and a teacher.

Power-packed Keynote

Setting the perfect context to the theme is our radical keynote speaker, Mansoor Khan, National Award winning film director and author of ‘The Third Curve-The End of Growth As We See It.’ His opening session promises to provoke even the most moderate among the audience. Look forward to a power-packed day with immense possibilities of stimulating discussions, transformative moments and a lot of fun!

Focus Areas

The focus of LSI 2015 is infusing, managing, and sustaining innovation in an existing system by asking ourselves – is everything about the system wrong? Are there elements that seem to be working well and hence can be magnified or supported? What more is needed and how do we bring that in? Can we shift the conversations we have been having around change?

Shift in Opportunities

Today, the Indian education system is on path of innovation and change. Opportunities turn into initiatives when a participant in a system is prepared to receive and act upon them. Schools and classrooms where a person feels encouraged to question and experiment have come up with path-breaking initiatives. It has been observed that teachers allowed with more flexibility can provide more than just textbook knowledge, schools with more space can give more than just academics, and students with a little more encouragement can maximize their potential. Experimentation in the right direction has helped students understand better and shown promising results.

Shift in Leadership, Roles and Responsibilities

India is known as a country of pilots. Several hundred initiatives are taken up across schools every year, but very few sustain. One of the critical factors amiss, even where flexibility is present, seems to be lack of ownership. Cases where there has been system-wide ownership have proven to be successful. As we seek to decentralize the learning process, it becomes imperative that we redefine roles and responsibilities among various stakeholders of the education system, allowing each person to be a leader in her own right. There needs to be comfort with failure and rejection to be able to learn, iterate and make innovations work.

Shift in Relationships

Education as a process concerns all human relations that the learner associates with. Each of these influences the learning process to varying degrees, owing to varying ideologies and methodologies. Lack of synchronization between the different gears leads to accidental building, rebuilding, and demolition of concepts rather than their desired reinforcement. Cases where the students, teachers, management and parents have worked mutually closely have turned out to be considerably efficient and successful over those where all have worked closely with the learner, but in isolation with respect to each other.

Shift in Competence

A dynamic world calls for mechanisms that can build relevant competencies in students and teachers alike to face newer challenges. Developments in learning pedagogies coupled with advancements in institutional technology have paved way for game-changing learning models, the flipped learning being one such case in point. We need personalized learning models that are able to instill newer competencies with the assistance of technology to ensure better learning outcomes for students. Research has shown that the quality of learning is most affected by the quality of teachers and instruction. Schools need ways to source, build, and continuously upgrade their teaching talent.
